# Adaption Labs, January 2026

**TL;DR**. First month as a funded company. Closed on Feb 4 is still a week away at the time of writing; this is our January pre-close update to investors who have already signed subscription docs. Team at 8, pipeline forming on both product surfaces, hiring targets set.

## Where we are

We are writing this from a position of clarity rather than certainty. The thesis we took to market (efficient, adaptive intelligence that learns continuously, without expensive retraining) is the thesis we are building against, without drift. Sudip and I have spent most of January interviewing candidates and first-call prospects. The ratio of signal to noise is high on both sides.

## Metrics

- **Team**: 8 (founding duo plus 6 early hires across research and infra).
- **Targeting**: 15 team members by end of Q1. Roles skew ML research and systems, with the first GTM hire planned late in Q1.
- **Cash**: fully funded subject to close.
- **Adaptive Data**: two Fortune 500 financial services firms in early conversations exploring adaptive inference for their existing model pipelines. Neither is papered. Both inbound.
- **Adaptive Intelligence**: waitlist at 34 from light early signal (our landing page went up in December).

## What we shipped

- Internal v0 of the adaptive-data pipeline running end-to-end on a single customer dataset.
- Careers page live with locations across US, Canada, UK, Poland, Brazil, and India. Global hiring is not a phase; it is the operating model.
- First two research scientists start Feb 1.

## Where we are uncertain

- **Multilingual adaptation as differentiation**: we believe our approach, informed directly by Aya, is one of our strongest moats versus labs optimizing for English-only benchmarks. The commercial pull is still unproven.
- **Enterprise cycle length**: the two finserv conversations are genuinely promising but we do not yet know whether a pilot will close in Q1, Q2, or at all.
